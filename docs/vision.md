# Product Vision

## Vision

Daily Flow Coach helps people build a workable day around the capacity they actually have. It combines flexible daily modules, a user-selected mode, and supportive AI coaching so users can make meaningful progress without being judged by an unrealistic schedule.

The long-term vision is a planning companion that understands both internal capacity and external commitments while keeping the user in control.

## Problem

Most daily planning tools emphasize fixed times, task volume, or streaks. Those approaches can break down when energy changes, care responsibilities arise, or an unexpected event reshapes the day. The result is often guilt, abandoned plans, and reduced trust in the tool.

Daily Flow Coach treats adaptation as a normal part of planning.

## Target users

- Working parents balancing professional and family responsibilities
- Caregivers supporting children, partners, elders, pets, or family members
- Professionals managing variable energy and demanding workloads
- Learners protecting time for ongoing development
- People who benefit from supportive structure without rigid scheduling

## User needs

Users need to:

- Choose a plan that reflects their current capacity
- See the day as a small set of understandable modules
- Adjust expectations without losing the whole plan
- Protect care, connection, movement, learning, and recovery alongside work
- Receive practical, low-pressure coaching
- Keep control over decisions made by AI
- Review the day with curiosity rather than judgment

## Product principles

1. **Capacity before optimization.** Start with the user's real energy and constraints.
2. **Flexible modules over rigid schedules.** Provide structure without false precision.
3. **User-selected mode is authoritative.** AI may recommend, but the user decides.
4. **Adapt the whole day.** Replanning should rebalance modules, not merely move one task.
5. **Care is broad and customizable.** Care / Connection must work across household and relationship contexts.
6. **Recovery is part of the plan.** Sleep, wind-down, and reduced expectations are valid outcomes.
7. **Support without pressure.** Language should be warm, clear, and nonjudgmental.
8. **Earn complexity.** Keep the MVP focused; add integrations only when they improve the validated experience.
